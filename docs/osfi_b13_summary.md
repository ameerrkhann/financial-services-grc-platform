# OSFI B-13 — Technology and Cyber Risk Management
## Quick Reference Summary
**Effective:** January 2024  
**Applies to:** All federally regulated financial institutions in Canada

---

## The 10 Key Requirements

| # | Requirement | What It Means |
|---|------------|---------------|
| 1 | Board ownership | Board formally owns and oversees cyber risk |
| 2 | Risk appetite | Written statement of acceptable cyber risk levels |
| 3 | Continuous assessment | Ongoing asset inventory, threat and vulnerability management |
| 4 | Critical systems | Identify and prioritise protection of critical systems |
| 5 | Control validation | Controls must be tested, not just documented |
| 6 | 24-hour notification | Significant incidents reported to OSFI within 24 hours |
| 7 | Third-party risk | Banks accountable for vendor-introduced cyber risk |
| 8 | Cloud requirements | Cloud services assessed for data residency and access risk |
| 9 | Operational resilience | Critical operations maintained during cyber incidents |
| 10 | Continuous improvement | Cyber risk maturity must improve over time with a roadmap |

## Key Terms

- **FRFI** — Federally Regulated Financial Institution (who B-13 applies to)
- **Technology risk** — risk of loss from technology failures or inadequacies
- **Cyber risk** — risk of loss from malicious digital attacks or unauthorised access
- **Risk appetite** — the level of risk an organisation is willing to accept
- **Operational resilience** — ability to deliver critical services through disruption
